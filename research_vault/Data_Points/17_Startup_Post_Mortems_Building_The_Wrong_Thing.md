# Startup Post-Mortems: Building the Wrong Thing

## The Number One Cause of Death
Across virtually all startup post-mortems, the most common refrain is: "We failed to understand our users and built the wrong thing." 
- This stems directly from a **Lack of Product-Market Fit**, which is a symptom of relying on founders' intuition rather than hard, continuous user feedback loops.
- Skipping comprehensive testing to "save resources" leads to heavily engineered products that the market actively rejects.

## The Webvan Fallacy
Startups often fall in love with the "idea" and ignore the user. They treat UX research as a one-time event before Launch, rather than a continuous CI/CD pipeline requirement.

Founders use Phantom because they are terrified of building the wrong thing.
