# Pricing Models in B2B SaaS Testing Software

## Industry Standards
SaaS companies targeting User Testing and QA generally utilize these pricing models:

1. **Tiered Packages (Industry Average: 3.5 Tiers):** Basic, Pro, Enterprise bounds. E.g., UserTesting ($15k+ annual enterprise tiers).
2. **Per-Seat Pricing:** Charges per individual user license. **Warning:** Common in older models, but heavily prone to password sharing and high churn in modern DevTool startups.
3. **Usage-Based / Pay-as-you-go:** Customers pay exactly for consumption (e.g., Number of testing minutes, Number of test executions).
4. **Credit-Based Systems:** Models like BetaTesting charge X credits per tester. Niche targeting or longer sessions cost more credits.

## Evolving Dynamics
- **Freemium is an Acquisition Strategy, Not a Business Model:** Free tier is strictly for lead gen.
- **Value-Based Pricing:** Pricing based on the perceived value delivered (e.g., $X saved in engineering hours or focus group recruitment fees).

## Actionable Phantom Takeaway
For Phantom, a **Usage-Based or Credit-Based System** is the most optimal fit. We are selling *compute and intelligence*, akin to an API call. 
- Charging per "Ghost Session" or per "Seance" directly aligns the cost of our underlying LLM/Playwright compute with the customer's perceived value.
- We should avoid per-seat pricing, as we want the *entire engineering team* actively collaborating on the Phantom Command Center dashboard. We monetize usage, not headcount.
